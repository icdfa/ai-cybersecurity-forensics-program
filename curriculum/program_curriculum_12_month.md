# Advanced AI in Cybersecurity & Digital Forensics Fellowship - Curriculum v3.0

## Program Philosophy

This 12-month, fully funded fellowship is a research-intensive program designed to cultivate the next generation of leaders, researchers, and innovators at the intersection of AI and security. This is not a training course; it is a deep, immersive experience focused on original research, hands-on application with enterprise-grade tools, and contribution to the field.

## Program Structure: 3 Semesters, 24 Modules

The curriculum is divided into three semesters, each with a distinct focus, comprising a total of 24 advanced modules.

--- 

### Semester 1: Core Foundations & Research Methodology (Months 1-4)

**Objective:** Build a world-class foundation in advanced mathematics, computer science, and research principles, fully leveraging our industry partnerships from day one.

*   **Module 1: Fellowship Orientation & Research Methodology**
    *   **Description:** Introduction to the fellowship, expectations, and the scientific method. Fellows learn how to conduct literature reviews, formulate research questions, and structure academic papers.
    *   **Partner Integration:** Fellows receive their **GitHub Pro accounts** and are introduced to GitHub for collaborative research and version control.

*   **Module 2: Advanced Python for Scientific Computing & Security**
    *   **Description:** Beyond basic scripting. Focus on high-performance Python with NumPy, SciPy, and Pandas for large-scale data analysis. Includes sessions on secure coding practices for Python.
    *   **Lab:** Performance analysis of different data processing techniques on a multi-gigabyte security dataset.

*   **Module 3: Enterprise Networking & Security Architecture (Cisco Partner Curriculum)**
    *   **Description:** Deep dive into network protocols, routing, switching, and security architecture using the official **Cisco Networking Academy** curriculum.
    *   **Partner Integration:** Extensive hands-on labs using **Cisco Packet Tracer** to design and secure complex enterprise networks. Prepares fellows for **CCNA certification**.

*   **Module 4: Cloud Security Engineering (AWS Academy & Microsoft Azure Labs)**
    *   **Description:** Architecting and securing cloud environments. Covers identity and access management (IAM), network security in the cloud, and serverless security.
    *   **Partner Integration:** Fellows utilize their **free Microsoft Azure and AWS Academy accounts** to deploy and secure multi-tier applications, implementing security best practices from both providers.

*   **Module 5: Applied Mathematics for Machine Learning**
    *   **Description:** A rigorous, graduate-level course in the essential mathematics for AI research: Linear Algebra, Vector Calculus, Probability, and Statistics.
    *   **Research Component:** Fellows will replicate and present the mathematical foundations of a foundational AI security paper.

*   **Module 6: Core Machine Learning & Statistical Analysis (Azure ML Studio)**
    *   **Description:** From theory to practice. Covers regression, classification, clustering, and dimensionality reduction. Emphasis on model selection and evaluation.
    *   **Partner Integration:** Fellows use **Microsoft Azure ML Studio** to build, train, and evaluate models, providing experience with enterprise-grade MLOps platforms.

*   **Module 7: Deep Learning & Neural Network Architectures (AWS SageMaker)**
    *   **Description:** A deep dive into neural networks, including CNNs for image analysis and RNNs/LSTMs for sequence data. Covers modern architectures like Transformers.
    *   **Partner Integration:** Fellows use **AWS SageMaker** for distributed training of deep learning models on large datasets, leveraging cloud GPU resources.

*   **Module 8: AI Ethics, Governance & Legal Frameworks**
    *   **Description:** In-depth analysis of ethical challenges in AI, including bias, fairness, and accountability. Covers global legal frameworks like the NIST AI RMF and the EU AI Act.
    *   **Research Component:** Fellows will write a position paper on a contemporary AI ethics issue.

--- 

### Semester 2: AI for Defensive Security & Forensics (Months 5-8)

**Objective:** Master the application of AI to build, automate, and enhance defensive security postures and forensic investigations.

*   **Module 9: AI-Powered Threat Intelligence & Proactive Threat Hunting**
    *   **Description:** Using AI to automate the collection, analysis, and correlation of threat intelligence. Develop models for proactive threat hunting in large datasets.
    *   **Lab:** Build a threat intelligence platform that uses NLP to extract indicators of compromise (IoCs) from security blogs and social media.

*   **Module 10: Intelligent Malware Analysis & Reverse Engineering**
    *   **Description:** Combining traditional reverse engineering with AI. Use machine learning to classify malware families, identify packed executables, and predict malware behavior.
    *   **Research Component:** Fellows will attempt to improve upon a state-of-the-art malware classification model.

*   **Module 11: AI for Advanced Network Defense & Intrusion Detection**
    *   **Description:** Building next-generation NIDS using deep learning. Focus on anomaly detection in encrypted traffic and identifying sophisticated, low-and-slow attacks.
    *   **Partner Integration:** Labs will be deployed on virtual networks within **Microsoft Azure/AWS** to simulate enterprise traffic.

*   **Module 12: AI-Enhanced Digital Forensics & Large-Scale Investigation**
    *   **Description:** Using AI to automate the analysis of forensic artifacts from disk and memory. Focus on scaling investigations across hundreds of compromised hosts.
    *   **Lab:** Develop an AI tool to automatically identify and categorize malicious activity from a full memory dump.

*   **Module 13: Natural Language Processing for Security (Log Analysis, Phishing Detection)**
    *   **Description:** Advanced NLP techniques (e.g., BERT, GPT) for security applications. Includes analyzing unstructured logs, detecting malicious emails, and identifying disinformation campaigns.
    *   **Research Component:** Fellows will develop a novel NLP model for a specific security task.

*   **Module 14: AI-Driven Security Orchestration (SOAR) & Automation**
    *   **Description:** Designing and implementing intelligent SOAR playbooks. Use reinforcement learning to optimize incident response workflows.
    *   **Lab:** Build a SOAR system that integrates with other lab projects (e.g., malware analysis, threat intel) to create a fully automated response pipeline.

*   **Module 15: Research Seminar I: State-of-the-Art in Defensive AI**
    *   **Description:** A weekly seminar where fellows present and critique recent papers from top security conferences (e.g., USENIX Security, ACM CCS, NDSS).

*   **Module 16: Mid-Program Research Project & Presentation**
    *   **Description:** Fellows work in small groups on a significant research project in defensive AI, culminating in a formal presentation and paper.

--- 

### Semester 3: Offensive AI, Advanced Research & Capstone Thesis (Months 9-12)

**Objective:** Pivot to an offensive mindset to understand and mitigate next-generation threats, culminating in a significant, publishable research project.

*   **Module 17: Adversarial Machine Learning: Theory & Practice**
    *   **Description:** A comprehensive study of attacks against ML models, including evasion, poisoning, and model extraction attacks.
    *   **Lab:** Implement a suite of adversarial attacks and test them against models built in previous modules.

*   **Module 18: Red Teaming & Security Validation of AI Systems**
    *   **Description:** Develop a formal methodology for red teaming AI systems. Includes vulnerability assessment, risk scoring, and reporting.
    *   **Partner Integration:** Fellows use **GitHub Copilot** to assist in writing attack scripts and analyzing model code for vulnerabilities.

*   **Module 19: Generative AI Threats & Deepfake Forensics**
    *   **Description:** Understanding the security risks of large language models (LLMs) and generative AI. Includes prompt injection, model hijacking, and advanced deepfake detection.
    *   **Research Component:** Develop a novel detection technique for AI-generated text or media.

*   **Module 20: AI for Vulnerability Research & Exploit Development**
    *   **Description:** Using AI techniques like fuzzing and symbolic execution to automatically discover vulnerabilities in software. Includes using AI to assist in exploit development.
    *   **Lab:** Build an AI-powered fuzzer and use it to find a zero-day vulnerability in a target application.

*   **Module 21: Research Seminar II: The Future of Offensive AI**
    *   **Description:** A weekly seminar focused on emerging and future threats from offensive AI, including autonomous hacking agents and AI-driven cyber warfare.

*   **Module 22: Capstone Thesis I: Proposal & Initial Research**
    *   **Description:** Fellows finalize their individual capstone thesis proposals and begin their literature review and initial experiments.

*   **Module 23: Capstone Thesis II: Development & Implementation**
    *   **Description:** Intensive development phase where fellows implement their capstone research project, with weekly one-on-one mentorship.

*   **Module 24: Capstone Thesis III: Final Paper, Defense & Industry Presentation**
    *   **Description:** Fellows write their final thesis in the format of a publishable academic paper, defend their research in a formal presentation, and present their findings to our industry partners.
