## Career Pathways

This program prepares you for a variety of in-demand roles at the intersection of AI and cybersecurity. The following pathways illustrate potential career progressions.

### Pathway 1: AI Security Specialist

- **Entry-Level:** AI Security Analyst
- **Mid-Level:** AI Red Teamer, AI Security Engineer
- **Senior-Level:** Principal AI Security Researcher, Head of AI Security

### Pathway 2: AI-Powered SOC Analyst

- **Entry-Level:** Junior SOC Analyst (with AI skills)
- **Mid-Level:** Senior SOC Analyst, Threat Hunter
- **Senior-Level:** SOC Manager, Director of Threat Intelligence

### Pathway 3: Digital Forensics Investigator

- **Entry-Level:** Junior Forensic Analyst
- **Mid-Level:** Digital Forensics Investigator, Incident Responder
- **Senior-Level:** Lead Forensic Investigator, Manager of Incident Response

---

## Job Role Descriptions

### AI Security Analyst

- **Responsibilities:** Monitor AI systems for vulnerabilities, analyze adversarial attacks, develop AI-based detection models.
- **Required Skills:** Python, Scikit-learn, TensorFlow, threat modeling, data analysis.
- **Average Salary (US):** $90,000 - $120,000

### AI Red Teamer

- **Responsibilities:** Conduct penetration testing of AI systems, develop adversarial attack techniques, assess model robustness.
- **Required Skills:** Adversarial ML libraries (ART), Python, penetration testing tools, strong understanding of ML model internals.
- **Average Salary (US):** $110,000 - $150,000

### Digital Forensics Investigator

- **Responsibilities:** Use AI tools to analyze large datasets, automate evidence processing, identify attack patterns.
- **Required Skills:** Forensic tools (Volatility, Autopsy), Python, data analysis, legal and ethical principles.
- **Average Salary (US):** $85,000 - $130,000

## Interview Preparation

### Common Technical Questions

- Explain the difference between a white-box and black-box adversarial attack.
- How would you use machine learning to detect phishing emails?
- Describe the steps you would take to investigate a compromised system.

### Behavioral Questions

- Describe a time you had to solve a complex security problem.
- How do you stay up-to-date with the latest security threats?
- How do you handle high-pressure situations like a security incident?

## Certification Pathways

This program provides a strong foundation for the following certifications:

- **CompTIA Security+:** Validates baseline security skills.
- **Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH):** Focuses on penetration testing.
- **Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP):** Advanced security management certification.
- **GIAC Certifications:** Specialized technical certifications.

A detailed mapping of the curriculum to these certifications is available in `career/certification_mapping.md`.

- **LinkedIn:** Maintain a professional profile and connect with industry professionals.
