# 12-Month Intensive Program: Detailed Weekly Curriculum

## Quarter 1: Foundations of AI and Cybersecurity (Months 1-3)

| Month | Week | Topics | Lab Assignment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | 1-2 | **Python for Cybersecurity:** Advanced scripting, data manipulation with Pandas, network programming with Scapy. | Automating network scanning and packet analysis. |
| 1 | 3-4 | **Fundamentals of AI & ML:** Supervised vs. Unsupervised learning, regression, classification, clustering. | Building a basic intrusion detection model. |
| 2 | 5-6 | **Data for Threat Intelligence:** Collecting and processing security data (logs, alerts), feature engineering. | Developing a threat intelligence data pipeline. |
| 2 | 7-8 | **ML for Threat Prediction:** Time-series analysis for attack forecasting, anomaly detection algorithms. | Predictive modeling of cyber attack trends. |
| 3 | 9-10 | **Static & Dynamic Malware Analysis with AI:** Feature extraction from binaries, behavior analysis with sandboxing. | AI-powered malware feature extraction. |
| 3 | 11-12 | **Deep Learning for Malware Detection:** CNNs for image-based malware classification, RNNs for behavioral analysis. | Building a deep learning malware classifier. |

**Q1 Mini-Project:** Develop an end-to-end intelligent malware detection system with a user-friendly interface.

## Quarter 2: AI-Powered Digital Forensics and Incident Response (Months 4-6)

| Month | Week | Topics | Lab Assignment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 4 | 13-14 | **Principles of Digital Forensics:** Evidence handling, chain of custody, forensic imaging and analysis. | Forensic analysis of a compromised system image. |
| 4 | 15-16 | **AI for Automated Evidence Collection:** Automating data acquisition from endpoints and cloud environments. | Building an AI agent for evidence triage. |
| 5 | 17-18 | **AI for Timeline Reconstruction:** Correlating events from multiple data sources to build attack timelines. | Automated attack timeline generation. |
| 5 | 19-20 | **NLP for Forensic Analysis:** Analyzing text-based evidence (emails, chat logs) for sentiment and intent. | Forensic analysis of a corporate espionage case. |
| 6 | 21-22 | **Computer Vision in Forensics:** Analyzing images and videos for evidence, steganography detection. | Detecting tampered images and hidden data. |
| 6 | 23-24 | **AI-Driven Incident Response (SOAR):** Integrating AI into Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response. | Developing a SOAR playbook for automated response. |

**Q2 Mini-Project:** Create an AI-powered forensic analysis tool to investigate a complex data breach scenario.

## Quarter 3: Offensive AI and Adversarial Machine Learning (Months 7-9)

| Month | Week | Topics | Lab Assignment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 7 | 25-26 | **Adversarial AI Threat Landscape:** Understanding the taxonomy of attacks against ML systems. | Research and presentation on recent adversarial attacks. |
| 7 | 27-28 | **Evasion Attacks and Model Robustness:** Crafting adversarial examples (FGSM, PGD) to bypass detectors. | Bypassing a malware classifier with an evasion attack. |
| 8 | 29-30 | **Data Poisoning and Inference Attacks:** Corrupting training data, extracting sensitive information from models. | Executing a data poisoning attack on a learning model. |
| 8 | 31-32 | **Generative AI Threats:** Creating deepfakes, generating polymorphic malware, advanced phishing. | Building and detecting a deepfake video. |
| 9 | 33-34 | **AI Red Teaming Methodologies:** Simulating real-world attacks on AI systems to find vulnerabilities. | Planning and executing an AI red team exercise. |
| 9 | 35-36 | **Continuous AI Security Validation:** Automating the testing and validation of AI model security. | Building an automated AI security validation pipeline. |

**Q3 Mini-Project:** Conduct a full red team assessment of a production AI system and present findings.

## Quarter 4: AI Governance, Capstone, and Career Prep (Months 10-12)

| Month | Week | Topics | Lab Assignment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 10 | 37-38 | **AI Risk Management Frameworks:** Implementing NIST AI RMF, understanding the EU AI Act. | Developing an AI risk management plan for an organization. |
| 10 | 39-40 | **Ethical Hacking for AI:** Responsible disclosure of AI vulnerabilities, bug bounty programs for AI. | Participating in a simulated AI bug bounty program. |
| 11 | 41-44 | **Capstone Project - Part 1:** Project proposal, literature review, system design, and initial development. | Weekly progress reports and design document submission. |
| 12 | 45-48 | **Capstone Project - Part 2:** Full implementation, testing, performance evaluation, and final report. | Final project demonstration and defense. |
| 12 | 49-52 | **Career Development:** Resume workshops, mock interviews with industry professionals, portfolio development. | Creating a professional portfolio and career plan. |

**Final Capstone Project:** Students will work in teams to develop a novel AI-powered cybersecurity or digital forensics solution, addressing a real-world problem.
